What affects architectural rendering cost?

There is no reliable single price for architectural rendering because the work changes with the project brief. A useful quotation begins with the required views, the completeness of source material and the level of development required before image production can start.

Scope of the image package

Image count, interior or exterior conditions, view complexity, contextual requirements and requested resolution all change the production scope. Define the intended audience and required views before comparing quotations.

Source-material readiness

Current plans, elevations, dimensions, models and material direction reduce uncertainty. Missing or changing design information may require additional coordination before a visual can be produced.

Review requirements

Agree who consolidates feedback, when reviews happen and what changes are included in the confirmed scope. This makes production milestones clearer for both teams.
